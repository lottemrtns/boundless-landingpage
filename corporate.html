Marketing

Context of campaign and promotional activities

Boundless suggests a sense of limitless potential and opportunity, which could be appealing to expats who are looking for a way to find and maintain connections outside of their home country. The word "boundless" also suggests a sense of openness and inclusivity, which could reflect the brand's commitment to helping expats from all backgrounds thrive abroad.

As a brand we focus on helping expats maintaining relationships and connections, especially for expats who may be far away from their loved ones. We want to facilitate a fun and engaging way for expats to create new connections, for friends to reconnect, and create new shared memories and experiences through our PubQuizes.

To promote our brand and create awareness for our website, we developed a comprehensive social media strategy focused primarily on Instagram. With its visual format and strong community of expats and travelers, Instagram is a natural fit for our brand. To bring this strategy to life a content calendar was created. By doing this we got a good view of what the promotional activities were going to look like. In the first part of our campaign our goal was to create a sense of curiosity about our brand and its concept by posting 2-3 times a week on Instagram. We did this by sharing high quality images and graphics with teaser taglines and quotes that hint at our brand‚Äôs values and mission. Using relevant hashtags will attract new followers and increase our reach. The second part we focused on introducing our brand, its concept, and values by posting once a day on Instagram. We used clear and concise messaging that explains what our brand is all about and what makes us unique. By using persuasive language, we tried to get our target audience excited about our brand. In the third part we included a call to action to get our target audience to visit our website and engage with our brand further. Finally, we started posting consistently every day and building customer relationships.

In summary, the Boundless brand's campaign and promotional activities include a comprehensive social media strategy focused on Instagram, sharing authentic and relatable content. These activities are designed to achieve the brand's reach, affect and response objectives.

Firstly, in terms of its reach objective of increasing brand awareness among expat communities by 15% within the first six months of launch, Instagram is a highly effective channel for reaching a broad audience. By using hashtags, collaborating with influencers and other brands, and sharing engaging content, the Boundless brand can increase its visibility and reach more expats who may be interested in its services.

Secondly, in terms of its response objective of increasing website traffic and conversion rate by 25% within the first six months of launch, Instagram can be used to drive traffic to the brand's website. By including links to the website in posts and stories, the brand can encourage followers to visit the site and engage with its content. This can ultimately lead to increased conversions and customer satisfaction.

Thirdly, in terms of its affect objective of increasing positive brand sentiment among expats by 20% within the first six months of launch, Instagram is an ideal platform for sharing authentic and relatable stories and experiences from other expats. By sharing content that resonates with its target audience, the brand can establish a sense of community and trust, which can ultimately lead to a more positive brand sentiment.

Learning Points

In the process of creating and executing this marketing and communication campaign we learned about several topics. Understanding the target audience: Through research and analysis of our target audience, we gained a deeper understanding of their needs, interests, and behaviors. This helped us tailor the messaging and communication channels to resonate with them more effectively. Defining clear objectives: By setting clear objectives for our campaign, we established measurable goals and metrics to track our progress and success. This helped us identify what is working and what is not and adjust your strategy accordingly. Boosting creativity and innovation: Developing a unique and creative campaign through brainstorming and ideation sessions, generated fresh ideas and approaches. Instagram algorithms: As we posted our content on Instagram, we tried several different options like posting in the morning, afternoon or evening, and using different kinds of hashtags. Looking at the statistics we see how it affects the response to our posts. One thing that stands out is that the content posted in the morning got the most interaction and likes.

Future Planning

Looking back at the campaign so far, it was a decent combination of quality content and messaging and consistency of posting. Although we could have tried to engage more with the audience by making more use of stories, polls and questions to make it more interactive. Beyond the first start of our campaign, we will continue to create engaging content that aligns with our brand values and resonates with our target audience.

New objective: To build a strong and loyal community around our brand by consistently creating valuable content and engaging with our audience.

The content calendar for the future includes:

Part 1: Behind the scenes: Sharing images and stories of the team behind the brand, giving a glimpse into the process of creating the product and the values that guide the team's work.

Part 2: User-generated content: Encourage customers to share their experiences with the brand and repost their content, tagging the brand's account and using a specific branded hashtag.

Part 3: Expert insights: Collaborate with experts in the field to create informative and engaging content that provides value to the target audience. This can include blog posts, webinars, or social media takeovers.

Part 4: Seasonal content: Create content that relates to holidays, events or seasonal trends. This can include posts about how the brand's product can be used in different situations or incorporating seasonal themes into the brand's messaging.

Part 5: Product features: Highlight specific product features and benefits through product demonstration videos, tutorials, or customer testimonials.

Part 6: Giveaways: Host contests and giveaways to engage with the audience and reward loyal customers. This can include social media challenges, product giveaways or exclusive discounts.

Part 7: Influencer partnerships: Collaborate with influencers or brand ambassadors who align with the brand's values and can help reach a wider audience. This can include sponsored posts, takeovers, or product reviews.

Lean Canvas

Fill in at least 2 bullet points per building block and according to the rubrics.

Problem

  • List your top 3 problems you solve for your target group(s).
  • Describe briefly how the problems are solved now (existing alternatives).

Solution

  • Outline the brand solution(s) you have for the above problem(s).

Customer segments

  • List your target customers and/or users.
  • Describe briefly the characteristics of your ideal customer (early adopter, brand persona).

Unique value proposition

  • With a single, clear compelling message, state why your brand idea is different and worth paying attention to.

Unfair advantage

  • List the aspect(s) of your brand that cannot easily be copied, also called your sustainable competitive advantage(s).

Channels

  • List your main path to customers.
  • How do you reach them? What channel(s) do you use.

Key metrics

  • List the key numbers that tell you how your brand experience is doing.
  • For example amount of users, downloads, visitors, subscriptions, sales etc. Numbers you are able to measure.

Revenue streams

  • List your sources of revenue: describe the revenue model and the (different) revenue stream(s).

Cost structure

  • List your main costs: define the fixed and variable costs.
  • Calculate the cost per unit.

Services/products

  • State what your product and/or service is and how this contributes to your unique value proposition.
  • Clarify the fit between the product/service developed and the brand identity/brand image.
